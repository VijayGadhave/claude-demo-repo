# Top 5 Tourist Places in Pune, India

> A travel guide to the best attractions in Pune, the "Oxford of the East" — where history, culture, and modernity blend seamlessly.

---

## 1. 🏰 Shaniwar Wada

Built in 1736 by Bajirao Peshwa I, Shaniwar Wada is the iconic symbol of the Maratha Empire in Pune. This architectural masterpiece served as the seat of the Peshwa rulers and features massive gates, ornate wooden pillars, and sprawling garden complexes. Today it is a historically significant monument and a must-visit for anyone interested in Maratha history.

- **Location:** Shaniwar Peth, Pune
- **Timings:** 8:00 AM – 6:30 PM (daily)
- **Entry Fee:** ₹5 (Indians), ₹125 (Foreigners)

---

## 2. 🕌 Aga Khan Palace

A monument of national importance, the Aga Khan Palace is famous for its role in India's freedom struggle. Built in 1892, it served as a prison for Mahatma Gandhi, his wife Kasturba Gandhi, and other freedom fighters during the Quit India Movement. The palace features stunning Italian arches, beautifully maintained lawns, and a museum showcasing collectibles from the Indian war of independence.

- **Location:** Nagar Road, Pune
- **Timings:** 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM (daily)
- **Entry Fee:** ₹5 (Indians), ₹100 (Foreigners)

---

## 3. ⛩️ Sinhagad Fort

One of the most prominent forts in Pune, Sinhagad (formerly known as Kondhana) is perched atop a hill and offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape. The fort is famous for the legendary Battle of Sinhagad (1670) fought by Tanaji Malusare. It is a popular trekking and picnic destination for both locals and tourists.

- **Location:** Donaje Village, Haveli Taluka, Pune
- **Timings:** Open all day
- **Entry Fee:** ₹30 (Indians)

---

## 4. 🛕 Dagdusheth Halwai Ganapati Temple

One of the most visited and revered temples in Pune, the Dagdusheth Halwai Ganapati Temple is dedicated to Lord Ganesha. Built in the late 19th century by a sweet seller named Dagdusheth Halwai, the temple attracts thousands of devotees year-round, including celebrities and dignitaries. The main Ganesh idol is insured for ₹10 million and the temple becomes especially vibrant during the annual Ganeshotsav festival.

- **Location:** Budhwar Peth, Pune
- **Timings:** 6:00 AM – 11:00 PM (daily)
- **Entry Fee:** Free

---

## 5. ⛪ Osho International Meditation Resort

Located in the upscale Koregaon Park area, the Osho International Meditation Resort is a world-famous spiritual retreat that attracts visitors from across the globe. Spread over 28 acres of lush greenery, it offers a variety of meditation programs, therapies, and courses inspired by the teachings of Osho (Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh). It is a unique blend of luxury, spirituality, and wellness.

- **Location:** 17 Koregaon Park, Pune
- **Timings:** 9:00 AM – 9:30 PM (daily)
- **Entry Fee:** Varies by program/activity

---

## 🗺️ Tips for Visiting Pune

- **Best Time to Visit:** October to February (pleasant cool weather)
- **Getting Around:** Pune has good auto-rickshaw, cab (Ola/Uber), and local bus services
- **Local Food to Try:** Misal Pav, Puran Poli, Vada Pav, Mastani (thick milkshake)
- **Language:** Marathi is the local language; Hindi and English are widely understood
